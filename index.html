Abstract

According to the WHO, around 433 million people have diabetes, and 1.5 million deaths are directly attributed to diabetes each year (R S1).

Diabetes is one of the fastest-growing chronic diseases in the world. In diabetic patients, the body loses the ability to maintain BG levels, causing multiple health complications. Several factors like physical exercise, carbohydrate intake, and stress affect the BG level in a patient (R Book, BG). A patient’s insulin dosage must be adjusted based on the current BG level, and a predicted value of the BG level (based on several factors such as carbohydrate intake, physical activity, etc) to prevent hyperglycaemia and mainly, hypoglycaemia. Therefore, an algorithm to accurately predict the BG level within the shortest Prediction Horizon is critical in efficient diabetes management.

The thesis intends to develop a BG prediction algorithm based on a patient’s carbohydrate intake and insulin dosage. A LSTM model (ML-based approach) is developed to analyse the clinical data provided and use a time-series prediction paradigm to predict BG levels. The accuracy of the model across different prediction horizons is calculated using several performance metrics. Clinical data provided by the NHS was used to run experiments to determine the prediction accuracy over increasing Prediction Horizons. The code includes an API that allows researchers/users to interact with the model. Lastly, a web app has been provided to produce graphical visualisations for each patient.

The project aims to interpret the results of the experiment and prove resourceful to researchers in the development of efficient BG prediction algorithms.

Features

View continuous blood glucose data

Analyse past BG and Insulin patters

Predict blood glucose value for different horizons

Warn about possible drop in blood glucose in the near future
